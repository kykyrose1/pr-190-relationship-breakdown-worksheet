Activity Overview

Influencer partnerships do not fail because of one single mistake. They fail because of accumulated breakdowns in communication, expectations, and trust.

In this activity, you will analyze a realistic influencer-brand partnership scenario and evaluate what went wrong, where the relationship broke down, how the situation could escalate into a PR issue, and how the brand should respond and prevent future issues.

This is a PR analysis exercise — focus on reputation, trust, and communication, not just marketing performance.

Learning Objectives

  • Apply relationship management concepts to real-world scenarios
  • Identify early warning signs of partnership failure
  • Evaluate how operational issues can escalate into reputational damage
  • Propose strategic, PR-informed solutions

Instructions

  • Work individually or in groups of 2–3
  • Read the scenario carefully before answering
  • Answer all questions in complete sentences

Scenario: Influencer Partnership Breakdown

A skincare brand partners with a mid-tier beauty influencer for a paid TikTok and Instagram campaign.

The Agreement Includes
  • 2 TikTok videos
  • 1 Instagram Reel
  • Posting within a 2-week timeframe
What Happens
  1. The brand sends a vague brief with minimal creative direction
  2. The influencer submits the first draft on time
  3. The brand responds 5 days later with extensive revision requests:
    • Changing the tone of the video
    • Adding scripted language
    • Refilming multiple scenes
  4. The influencer pushes back — the changes feel inauthentic to their audience
  5. The brand insists on revisions and continues to delay approval
  6. The posting deadline passes
  7. The influencer posts on Instagram Stories: "Brands need to respect creators' voices. Not everything needs to sound like an ad."
  8. Followers begin speculating about the brand
  9. Comments appear questioning the brand's professionalism
